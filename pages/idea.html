Smart building

Smart buildings use advanced technology to stay safe during earthquakes. When the ground starts to shake, special sensors instantly detect the movement and activate safety systems. The building automatically shuts off gas lines to prevent fires and stops elevators at the nearest floor so people can exit safely. Some smart buildings even have giant computer-controlled weights and shock absorbers at the base that move to balance the building and reduce shaking. After the earthquake, built-in sensors quickly check the walls and beams for hidden damage and send real-time safety alerts to everyone's smartphones.

Energy saving tips in summer

Find ways to save energy, keep costs down, and stay comfortable when the weather warms up.To keep your home cool and save money, close window coverings during the day and seal cracks around doors and windows to keep hot air out. Set your thermostat as close to the outdoor temperature as comfortable, raise it when you leave, and keep lamps or TVs away from it so it doesn't run extra hours. Turn off ceiling fans when you leave the room since they cool people rather than spaces, and use exhaust fans to vent kitchen and bathroom humidity outside. Finally, maintain your cooling system by scheduling regular tune-ups and keeping vents vacuumed, run your dishwasher and laundry only with full loads, and lower your water heater to 120 degrees to cut down on energy costs.

Eco Transport

Eco transport, or green transportation, means using ways of traveling that do not hurt the environment. It focuses on reducing air pollution and saving energy by moving away from traditional gas-powered cars. Common examples include walking, riding bicycles, using electric trains, and driving electric or hybrid vehicles. By using renewable energy like electricity or solar power, eco transport helps keep the air clean and fights climate change. Choosing these green options makes cities quieter, healthier, and much better places to live.

Quality air

Clean air and green spaces like parks work together to keep cities healthy. Trees and plants act like natural filters that clean the air by trapping dust and absorbing pollution. They also make fresh oxygen for us to breathe and cool down hot streets, which stops dirty smog from forming. When a city has clean air and nice parks, people walk and exercise more, leading to fewer breathing problems like asthma. These quiet green places also help people relax, lowering stress and making everyone feel happier.
